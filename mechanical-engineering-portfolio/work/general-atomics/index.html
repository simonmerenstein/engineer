Project Scope

The internship combined large-assembly CAD work, mechanical testing, data analysis, and hardware design under strict information-control constraints.

  • Harness routingRouted harnessing through a 5000+ part satellite dispenser assembly using SolidWorks.
  • Interference avoidanceChecked routing paths, avoided clashes, located cable tie anchors, and estimated total wiring weight.
  • Release mechanismDesigned a satellite release mechanism concept and supported testing with a mass simulator.
  • Nut factor testingTested and analyzed different bolt sizes to correlate clamp load to torque values.
  • LightweightingUsed preload understanding to support lighter bolt design while maintaining preload requirements.
  • Vibration validationEvaluated release mechanism effects during vibration-table testing.

Transferable Results

Even without showing hardware, the work demonstrates large-assembly ownership and test-backed mechanical design.

  • Integrated harness routes through dense assembly constraints while accounting for anchors and mass.
  • Used physical test data to connect torque, clamp load, nut factor, and lightweighting decisions.
  • Supported vibration-table evaluation of mechanism behavior using a representative mass simulator.
  • Practiced communicating mechanical design decisions in a constrained, high-accountability environment.
