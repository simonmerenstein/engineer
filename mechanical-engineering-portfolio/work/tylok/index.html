Work / Tylok

Manufacturing Tooling and Test Fixtures

Manufacturing engineering co-op work at Tylok Fittings and Valves, focused on production-floor tooling, machine improvements, testing concepts, and fixtures for fittings and valves.

Tylok Fittings and Valves Manufacturing Engineering Co-op January-July 2023 SolidWorks / fabrication / testing
CADSolidWorks models for 3D printed and metal fabricated production tools.
FixturesAssembly wheels, tester parts, storage aids, and machine improvement hardware.
TestTube rotation testing and hydraulic impulse tester concept development.
FloorProjects driven by operator safety, efficiency, and machine performance.

Scope

The co-op combined quick-turn design work with practical shop-floor constraints: understand the production problem, create a manufacturable tool or fixture, support fabrication, and validate fit/use.

  • Hydromat chip cart wallDesigned and supported a fabricated machine-area improvement for chip containment and workflow.
  • Burst testersWorked on tester-related hardware for fittings and valves.
  • Stock storage containerSupported production supply organization with designed storage improvements.
  • Tube rotation testingCompared tube slip/rotation behavior, including Tylok versus Swagelok testing.
  • Assembly wheelsSpearheaded an assembly wheel to improve safety and efficiency in fittings assembly.
  • Impulse testerCollaborated on a hydraulic impulse tester concept to predict lifetime of fittings and valves.

Results

The co-op work produced production-floor tools and tester concepts with practical value for operators, maintenance, and manufacturing engineering.

  • Created SolidWorks designs for 3D printed and metal fabricated parts used to improve machine performance.
  • Led the design, manufacturing, and construction of an assembly wheel for safer and more efficient fitting assembly.
  • Collaborated on hydraulic impulse tester work intended to predict fitting and valve lifetime.
  • Contributed to additional production support projects including a Davenport stamp cover, elbow gauge, Machine Metric software work, and eye wash prints.
